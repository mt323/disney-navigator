# Disneyland & DCA Wait Time Statistics

## Data Source & Methodology

All wait time data in this document is sourced from **[queue-times.com](https://queue-times.com)**, which tracks live and historical queue times for theme parks worldwide. The statistics represent **historical averages compiled from crowd-sourced and API-reported wait times** over multiple years of data collection (generally 2014--2026).

**Important caveats:**
- These are **approximate figures based on historical averages** and do not guarantee actual wait times on any given day.
- Real-time conditions (ride breakdowns, weather, special events, seasonal overlays) can cause significant deviations.
- Hourly data reflects all-time averages across all days; actual patterns on a specific Monday in March may differ.
- Data was retrieved on March 8, 2026.

**Target visit date: Monday, March 16, 2026**

## Key Insights for March 16 Planning

### March Is a Busier-Than-Average Month
- DCA crowd levels in March average **61%** -- the **busiest month of the year** for that park.
- DL March crowd levels are elevated but not peak. Radiator Springs Racers hits its annual high of **81 min** average in March.
- Spring Break crowds may overlap with mid-March dates, increasing wait times beyond historical averages.

### Monday Is Not a "Quiet" Day
- Contrary to common advice, **Monday is historically one of the busiest days** at Disneyland, averaging **58% crowd level** (tied with Saturday for highest).
- Several rides show Monday as their busiest weekday: Space Mountain (49 min), Smuggler's Run (36 min), Rise of the Resistance (67 min), Star Tours (27 min), Mr. Toad's (17 min), Buzz Lightyear (20 min).
- This may be driven by visitors who arrive for weekend trips and stay through Monday, or by annual passholders who target "off-peak" days that are no longer truly off-peak.

### Optimal Timing Strategy
1. **Rope Drop (8:00--9:30 AM):** Prioritize the highest-wait rides. Rise of the Resistance and Radiator Springs Racers see their lowest waits in the first 60--90 minutes, but even then expect 45--60+ min for RotR.
2. **Midday (11 AM--4 PM):** The worst window for all rides. Use this time for dining, shows, low-wait rides (Mr. Toad, Buzz Lightyear, Pirates), or Lightning Lane purchases.
3. **Evening (7 PM--close):** The second-best window. Many rides see 30--50% reductions from peak. Rise of the Resistance drops to ~48 min after 9 PM. Tiana's Bayou Adventure drops dramatically to 10--20 min near closing.

### Year-over-Year Trend Is Favorable

### High-Capacity Rides as Schedule Fillers
- **Pirates of the Caribbean** (20--30 min) and **Mr. Toad's Wild Ride** (15--20 min) maintain short waits even on busy days due to high throughput or short ride duration. These are good midday options.
- **Star Tours** (20--35 min) and **Buzz Lightyear** (15--25 min) are also solid midday choices.

### Single Rider Lines Available
- **Radiator Springs Racers**, **Incredicoaster**, and **Web Slingers** all offer single rider queues that can dramatically reduce wait times (often 50--70% shorter).
